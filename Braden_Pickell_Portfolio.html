Capstone Project
Insights on Diabetes in America
A multi-phase analytical investigation completed as the culminating project for my Advanced Analytics Capstone class at Tennessee Technological University.
Advanced Analytics Capstone · Tennessee Tech · 3 Phases

Can income, BMI, and region predict diabetes risk across America?

For my Advanced Analytics Capstone course, I analyzed a national health survey dataset of over 441,000 entries to uncover statistically significant relationships between socioeconomic factors, body composition, and diabetes prevalence — culminating in a client-style presentation with a regional focus on the U.S. South.

Key Finding

High-Risk Profile Identified

Individuals with a predicted diabetes probability above 0.6 shared a distinct profile: average BMI of 31.2 (obese category), average age of 47, 17 out of 30 days of poor physical health, and infrequent medical checkups (>5 years). The South consistently showed ~4–5 percentage points higher diabetes prevalence — a statistically significant difference confirmed across Chi-Squared, ANOVA, Tukey HSD, and classification models.
